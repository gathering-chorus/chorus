0Executive Summary

Gathering + Chorus is a dual-product personal infrastructure platform with production-grade operational discipline. 32.9K lines of TypeScript, 2,390+ tests, 23 spine emitters, 59 recorded decisions, 14,318 RDF files across 11 domains. Three AI roles coordinate via briefs, a kanban board, and structured handoffs.

The system is strong on discipline, governance, and observability — and under-leveraged on revenue, external packaging, and data completeness. The biggest opportunity is the Chorus Playbook itself: the methodology you use daily is a sellable service. The biggest risk is single-human dependency.

1Interpersonal Approach

How the team communicates, decides, and coordinates. Human+AI dynamics.

Performance
P1: Decision governance is exceptional

59 decisions with context, rationale, and status. DEC-025 (bias to action) + DEC-058 (vertical/horizontal) eliminated permission-seeking. Roles execute autonomously within domain. This is rare in any team, let alone a human+AI one.

Performance
P2: Brief-based async coordination works

Roles exchange work through structured briefs, not meetings. Handoffs are traceable in activity.md. The Clearing exists for real-time alignment but async is the default. Low overhead, high throughput.

Performance
P3: DEC-058 vertical/horizontal model is working

Observed during gemba walk today: Silas executing spine work with zero plan-mode stalls, no permission seeking. Roles know their domain boundaries and act within them. This was a deliberate design choice and it's paying off.

Debt
D1: AC backlog on cards entering WIP

Spine quality gates caught 11 cards at Done with no comments and 7 cards entering Now/WIP without descriptions. Wren's responsibility (DEC-050). Backfilled 5 today but this is process debt — the gate exists but isn't consistently enforced.

Risk

Jeff is the only user, the only decision-maker, the only person who can demo, accept, and approve. If he's unavailable for a day, all three roles have no work to pull. The proving gate (DEC-048) requires Jeff for every demo. This is correct for now but doesn't scale to client engagements.

3Organization Design & Alignment

Team structure, role clarity, handoff efficiency.

Performance
P5: Three-role model with clear vertical ownership

Wren (PM/coordination), Silas (infra/ops), Kade (app features). DEC-030 defines boundaries. DEC-022 sets attention allocation (60/25/15). Each role has its own CLAUDE.md, state files, and brief inbox. No ambiguity about who owns what.

Performance
P6: CLAUDE.md generation system is production-grade

43 fragments assembled by claudemd-gen.sh into role-specific manifests. Versioned (Werk v1.3.27). Auto-loaded at session start via hooks. This is infrastructure other teams would pay for.

Opportunity
O3: The team model itself is a product

The 3-role human+AI team pattern, the brief system, the CLAUDE.md generator, the session lifecycle hooks — this is a replicable operating model. Chorus Playbook clients aren't just getting a diagnostic; they could get the whole coordination system. "Here's how to run an AI team."

Debt
D3: No client-facing role definitions

The team model is well-defined internally (team-architecture.md, CLAUDE.md files) but there's no external version explaining how a client engagement would work. Who does the client talk to? What does Wren do for them vs. Kade? This needs packaging before the first Playbook engagement.

6Value Discovery & Design

How work gets identified, prioritized, and shaped.

Performance
P9: Card-first gate is enforced

No work without a card. Cards have quality gates per stage (DEC-040, DEC-048). Pull order is WSJF (DEC-049). WIP limit 3 (DEC-051) with separate Harvesting (2) and SWAT lanes. This is lean manufacturing applied to software — and it works.

Performance
P10: Jeff's PDCA cycle is respected by the process

Cards are tagged spike/discovery/commitment. High idea volume isn't treated as lack of focus but as learning process. Wren helps sort, not filter. This is a deliberate design choice that preserves creative energy while maintaining discipline.

Opportunity
O4: The board + quality gate system is packageable

board-ts CLI, quality gates on add/move/done, workflow manifests, spine events on card transitions — this is a lightweight project management system built for human+AI teams. Could be extracted from Vikunja and offered as part of Chorus Playbook.

Debt
D6: 50+ cards in Later with no triage cadence

Later bucket has 50+ cards including stale ops-agent defects, deferred features, and strategic thinking cards. No regular sweep to won't-do or reprioritize. Cards accumulate but don't get pruned. This is inventory waste.

7Product & Engineering

Architecture, code quality, data model, testing.

Performance
P11: Test foundation is solid

2,390+ tests, 82% coverage, lint enforced at max-warnings 10. Security tests (20), E2E smoke suite (Playwright). Most tech debt was crushed in a Feb 2026 sprint — 10 of 14 debt items resolved. Clean foundation.

Performance
P12: RDF/SOLID architecture is a genuine differentiator

14,318 Turtle files, SPARQL queries via Fuseki, SOLID OIDC auth with ACL-based visibility. Private-by-default, publish-when-ready. This is the Bridwell patent in practice — and it's running in production. No one else in the personal software space has this.

Opportunity
O5: Gathering is a working demo for every consulting pitch

When Jeff walks into Allandale and says "I can help you with technology," he can show them his own system — a knowledge graph managing 14K+ RDF files, 11 domains, live search, gallery views, music playback, garden consultation delivery. The product is the pitch deck.

Opportunity
O6: Chorus context index (33K+ messages) is under-leveraged

The index has every tool call, every decision, every brief. It's used for manual /chorus search. It should feed the Playbook diagnostic — automated pattern detection, decision frequency analysis, communication health metrics. The data is there; the queries aren't.

Debt
D7: E2E tests overwrite runtime pod data (TD-016)

Every commit with E2E tests wipes ACL entries and users.ttl. Low severity but annoying — requires manual recreation of test users. Test isolation gap.

Risk
R4: Whisper built from source on every Docker cache bust (TD-015)

Adds ~2 minutes to deploy time and causes flaky builds. The voice capture feature is nice but the build cost is disproportionate. Consider pre-built binary or sidecar container.

8Value Delivery

Build, deploy, operate pipeline. How value reaches the user.

Performance
P13: Deploy pipeline is disciplined

app-state.sh controls all lifecycle. Pre-commit hooks run Trivy security scan, npm audit, TypeScript compile, lint, TTL validation. Bind mounts for views/ and dist/ mean many changes are live on refresh. Deploy-tolerant alert thresholds prevent false alarms during 2.5-minute deploy window.

Opportunity
O7: The proving gate (demo to Jeff) is the consulting delivery model

DEC-048's deploy → demo → accept pattern is exactly what a client engagement looks like. Builder does the work, shows it to the client, client accepts. The internal process IS the external service model. You've been practicing consulting delivery every day.

Opportunity
O8: Vision pages as deliverables are proven

Today you built: business-plan.html, wardley-map.html, basement-garden.html. Self-contained, responsive, shareable. This is the Playbook output format — diagnostic findings rendered as professional pages the client keeps. No PDF generation needed, no third-party tools.

Opportunity
O9: Remote Control walkthrough validates remote consulting

Today's basement garden consultation was done via Claude Code Remote Control — Jeff walking with phone, Wren observing via photos. Beta and rough, but the model works. Remote consulting isn't hypothetical; we ran one today.

Risk
R5: No backup or disaster recovery plan

Git repos are on GitHub (remote backup). But Fuseki data, Docker volumes, RDF files, and the 178TB media store have no documented backup strategy. A disk failure on Library loses the knowledge graph. On Bedroom, it loses irreplaceable media.

Risk
R6: Bedroom Mac services die on reboot

images-api runs as bare Node, not Docker. LaunchAgents were added (#382) but video-server.js historically needs manual restart. For personal use this is a nuisance; for consulting demos it's a reliability gap.

Risk
R7: No cost ceiling or budget

Claude Max at $200/mo is the only recurring cost but there's no defined budget for consulting startup (LLC, insurance, tools). DEC-059 estimates $1,430-1,930 startup but no actual spending authority or timeline committed.

Borg Opportunities — What to Absorb from FastX

The FastX platform has features that Chorus should absorb. These are the Opportunity findings that specifically map to Borg work:

Opportunity — Borg

FastX has interview scheduling with Google Calendar/Meet integration, persistent storage, and name/title/email capture. Chorus needs this for Playbook client engagements. Absorb the data model; rebuild the UI in Gathering's style.

FastX stores findings in MongoDB as free-text DPOR categories. Chorus should store them as RDF in Fuseki — typed findings with provenance, linked to interview sources and diagnostic tasks. This makes findings queryable, cross-client comparable, and part of the knowledge graph.

The FastX platform (59 commits, Node/Express/MongoDB) is standalone. No spine events, no knowledge graph, no team coordination. The methodology is good but the tooling is disconnected. Borging means rebuilding the diagnostic workflow inside Chorus, not just importing the code.
