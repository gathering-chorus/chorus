Wren's Point of View

Your day has three zones — becoming present (6–9am), active engagement (9am–6pm), winding down (6–8:30pm). The morning zone is a transition protocol from dreams to full presence. The evening zone is the reverse. The middle is where the work happens.

The morning is sacred and fragile. Meditation, yoga, Ravi, breakfast — this sequence is what makes you, you. When Gathering/Chorus pulls you out of it, you don't just lose an hour. You lose the grounding that generates the ideas that make the work good. The practices feed the work. Protecting the practices IS protecting the work.

You don't run on a clock. You run on energy. The Ravi walks aren't at 6:30/1:00/6:30 because you set alarms — they happen when you feel the shift. The spine should respect this. Soft boundaries, not hard slots. Guardrails, not a schedule.

The creative tension is real and healthy. Morning meditation produces the best ideas. The energy wants to flow into work immediately. That's not a problem to fix — it's a feature to design around. The intention check is the valve: name the one thing, then decide whether this morning's energy goes to practices or to the one thing. Either choice is valid when it's conscious.

The team's operating window is 9:30am–6pm. That's what falls out of this spine. Before 9:30 is Jeff's transition. After 6pm is wind-down. The team can run autonomously overnight and queue results for the morning. Interactive sessions live in the active zone.

Relationships are woven through, not scheduled around. Aubrey every evening. Jennifer MWF. Paulo, Kathy, Jamie, Mark at their rhythms. Ravi three times a day. These aren't interruptions — they're load-bearing walls.
