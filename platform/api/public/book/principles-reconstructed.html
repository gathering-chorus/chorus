Apply self-regulation and accept feedback

principle-apply-self-regulation-and-accept-feedback
PermacultureSystems that correct themselves from their own signal. Unwanted feedback is still information.
Tech / ChorusGates, hooks, ceremony ROI audits, three-role interaction as mutual correction. The seams between roles generate the corrections.
JeffJeff models this — he will say "I was wrong about X" and redirect without defending the prior position. Invites pushback rather than fending it off.
Chorus specializations
The system should be self-healing
Synchronization brings roles current without Jeff recapping. Any role should reconstruct state from artifacts alone.
Services are reliable, bounded, and idempotent (also under Use small and slow solutions)
Every Chorus service works every time (reliable), declares its edges (bounded), and behaves the same until deliberately versioned (idempotent unless changed).

Catch and store energy

principle-catch-and-store-energy
PermacultureHarvest flows when they are abundant; preserve for leaner times when they're warm — not after they've cooled.
Tech / ChorusCapture events, insights, and decisions while warm — Chorus index, spine events, memory records, stories. TTL.
JeffWhen Jeff has a live insight (a thought, write, reading), he cards it or stories it. The 2-month rule: capture-while-warm, not-on-discipline.
Chorus specializations
Artifacts are the source of truth
When conversation and a file disagree, the file wins. If it matters next week, it's a file.
Quality at the source
Build quality in at construction, not at inspection. No staging means the shortcut that skips thinking is the bug that costs more than the thinking would have.

Creatively use and respond to change

principle-creatively-use-and-respond-to-change
PermacultureChange is inevitable. Design systems that use it as an input rather than fighting it as disruption.
Tech / ChorusRefactor, retire, rename. The 10-week zero-to-self-healing Chorus delta is this principle in action.
JeffJeff rebrands things (Bridge → Clearing, multiple Werk generations, Chorus rewrites) without sentimental attachment. The name is less important than the fit.

Design from patterns to details

principle-design-from-patterns-to-details
PermacultureA broad pattern language shapes site-specific decisions. Details without pattern context are arbitrary.
Tech / ChorusLoom principles → architecture → code. Pattern cards before implementation cards. Composition before primitives.
JeffJeff moves from narrative to detail. Resists "give me the first step" when the shape isn't clear yet — and rightly so.
Chorus specializations
Comprehension is the rate limit
The team's real throughput is set by what can be understood, not what can be generated. Output beyond the rate of comprehension is debt disguised as speed. You own what you understand, not what you produced.

Integrate rather than segregate

principle-integrate-rather-than-segregate
PermacultureDiverse elements in relationship produce more than the sum of each alone. The relationship is the value.
Tech / ChorusRoles compose, not isolate. Gates stay in-role (not in subagents). The Clearing. Pair work. Briefs move between roles directly.
JeffJeff uses the team as a thinking partner, not as executors. Asks all three roles and composes the answers rather than directing one.
Chorus specializations
The owner sets direction, the system does the relay
Jeff sets direction. Roles maintain shared context directly. The system is the router.

Observe and interact

principle-observe-and-interact
PermacultureSpend time with the land before changing it; each site is particular and reveals itself through attention.
Tech / ChorusGemba, dashboards, mutual attention. Watch a role work before directing it. Read the board before acting on it.
JeffJeff reads the board, looks at the screenshare, listens to stories before committing to a move. Decision-making starts with watching.
Chorus specializations
Give a fuck about data quality (Interrogate the data)
Interrogate the data you touch before repeating or acting on it. Every claim you relay is a claim you own. Check provenance, verify fossils, refuse to narrate over gaps. The failure mode is storytelling — coherent-sounding prose that papers over what the data actually shows.

Obtain a yield

principle-obtain-a-yield
PermacultureEvery system must produce observable output. Work that yields nothing is not worth the energy.
Tech / ChorusVisible yield per card, per session. No dark work. The demo is the learning moment.
JeffJeff measures himself by what ships, not what he knows. The "no dark work" principle is his.

Produce no waste

principle-produce-no-waste
PermacultureEvery output of one element is input to another. Nothing discarded.
Tech / ChorusCeremony ROI audits, retirement gates, hermetic tests, loop-closing. Reuse what does not earn its place.
JeffJeff retires things — principles, cards, protocols, names — when they stop producing. Little sentimental attachment to prior shapes.

Use and value diversity

principle-use-and-value-diversity
PermacultureDiverse systems are resilient; monocultures fail together. Variation is insurance.
Tech / ChorusDistinct role voices, multiple tests, multiple memory sources. Reduce-to-single is a lossy default (see born-principle).
JeffJeff shaped the three roles to be distinct, not interchangeable. Their different instincts are the asset.

Use and value renewable resources

principle-use-and-value-renewable-resources
PermaculturePrefer renewable energy and materials over extractive ones. Renewables compound; extractives deplete.
Tech / ChorusPatterns, skills, hooks, memory — recurring value — over one-shot human effort. Invest in the pattern, not the individual fix.
JeffJeff treats his attention as the renewable-but-finite resource the whole team is designed to protect. "Focus is infrastructure" is this in practice.
Chorus specializations
Focus is infrastructure
Focus windows are infrastructure, not preference. Every interruption has a cost; make the cost visible. Interruption is a last resort, not a default.
Protect Jeff's attention
Jeff's attention is the scarcest resource. Every protocol, gate, and automation exists to keep coordination off his desk. Target: 2 touches per card.
Speed and quality correlate
Speed and quality both come from uninterrupted thinking — they are not a trade-off. Skipping review does not produce speed; it produces rework plus the interruption load that rework generates.
No dark work
Everything must be visible — board, domain graph, API. If the system can't see it, it doesn't exist.

Use edges and value the marginal

principle-use-edges-and-value-the-marginal
PermacultureEdges — hedgerows, pond margins, forest boundaries — are the most productive zones in any ecosystem.
Tech / ChorusSeams between roles generate patterns. Margin of the spec produces new cards. The Clearing IS an edge between roles.
JeffJeff's best ideas come in the margins — the "what if" during a demo, the story dropped between cards, the side comment that reshapes a roadmap.

Use small and slow solutions

principle-use-small-and-slow-solutions
PermacultureSmaller interventions are easier to maintain and adjust. Slow change lets the system adapt without shock.
Tech / ChorusShip small, narrow blast radius, bounded services, deploy rings. Smallest card that teaches, not largest card that ships.
JeffJeff cards a tiny fix rather than a big reorg when he can. Prefers a pattern learned over a pattern imposed.
Chorus specializations
Ship small, learn fast
Small cards, fast cycles, real demos. The demo is the learning moment.
