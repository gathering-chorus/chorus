Ontology Bridge

How this connects to the Self ontology sketch — the three overlapping circles.

Sketch Region Ring Buddhist Concept What It Looks Like in Practice
The Self (large circle) Core Vedanā — feeling-tone of daily life Aubrey, Julian, Ravi. The felt texture of each day. Not chosen in the moment — always present.
Relationships (right circle) Inner Brahmavihāra — divine abodes (loving-kindness, compassion) Jennifer, Paulo, Kathy, Jamie, Mark. Love, support, learning. Where inner life meets other people.
Common Bond (inner circle) Core ∩ Inner Dhamma-vicaya — investigation of phenomena The overlap. Jennifer is meditation partner AND learning relationship. Teaching/Observing happens in every ring.
Outside Outer Saṅgha — community Community center, LLUG, financial advisor. The wider container. Shared space without intimate bond.
The overlap matters most. In the sketch, the Common Bond (Teaching/Observing) sits at the intersection of Self and Relationships. In practice, that's Jennifer — she's the only inner-ring person who shares Jeff's core practice (meditation) rather than a learning conversation. She's in both circles simultaneously. The ontology should model this overlap, not force people into one ring.

Wren's Point of View

Every person in the inner ring is a learning relationship. Jeff doesn't have social appointments — he has practice partners. Jennifer is meditation. Paulo, Kathy, Jamie, Mark are growth conversations. Aubrey is the daily ground. Ravi is the rhythm.

This isn't a contact list. It's a map of how Jeff stays in practice. The people ARE the practices.

The concentric rings also map to Jeff's "weather on the inside" insight. Core ring people are the climate — always present, always shaping conditions. Inner ring people are the weather systems — they move through the week with regularity, each one bringing a different quality. Outer ring is the season — slower rhythms that set the larger context.

For the Self ontology: jb:Person and jb:Relationship should be first-class classes, not subclasses of anything else. A relationship has a cadence, a ring, a practiceBinding, and a spineRole (anchor, companion, teacher, advisor). The ring determines trust and visibility. The practice binding connects people to the Practicing value stream.

The sketch already showed us: Relationships aren't outside the Self. They're the right circle, overlapping with the left. The Common Bond at the center — Teaching/Observing — is what every relationship in every ring is doing, at different depths.
