This isn't borrowed philosophy — it's convergent discovery through lived practice. That's what makes it authentic material for an ontology.

4. Connecting to the Value Stream

The sketch's three regions map onto the value stream as the inner experience of each stage:

Value Stream Stage Sketch Region Inner Experience
Spark (noticing) Perception — Attention Something catches awareness. Desiring-Attention reaches toward it.
Capture (preserving) Volition — Doing The intention to hold what was noticed. An act of will.
Triage (sorting) Perception — Investigating "What is this?" Discrimination, labeling, first meaning-making.
Settle (placing) Form — Holdings / Objects It has a home. The material world organized.
Revisit (returning) PracticeCore — Observing Coming back to what was placed. Teaching yourself through re-seeing.
Transform (growing) Volition — Energy Things change form. Lifecycle transitions are visible evidence of growth.
Connect (relating) Volition — Connections + Relationship circle Relationships emerge between things. Where self meets other.
Reflect (understanding) Awareness — Structurality Stepping back to see the pattern. Consciousness observing itself.
The connection: The value stream (Sow → Cultivate → Harvest → Gather → Reflect) isn't just an information flow. It mirrors the Buddhist path of attention: noticing → investigating → placing → connecting → understanding. The sketch is the inner experience of what the app does outwardly.
