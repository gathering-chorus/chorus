# Jeff's Practice Spine

## Morning Block (6:00–8:30am goal)

**This is a transition protocol, not just a checklist.** Jeff sleeps well and dreams heavily. Waking state is diffuse — needs time to come into focus. Each step moves him from dream-like to fully active:

meditation (grounding) → yoga (embodiment) → walk Ravi (movement + air) → breakfast (fuel) → **fully present**

Skipping steps doesn't just lose practice time — it compromises the transition. Jeff starts the day less present, which affects everything after.

Intention: done by **8:30am**. Reality: Gathering/Chorus work often pulls Jeff out of this sequence. Practices get delayed into afternoon or skipped. This is the primary tension the calendar needs to protect.

## Notes
- Morning block is the most fragile. Team work (Gathering/Chorus) is the main disruptor.
- When morning practices get skipped, they compound — Jeff loses the grounding that makes the rest of the day work.
- The team should NOT be competing with the morning block. No notifications, no briefs needing response before 9am.
- **The creative tension**: Morning practices (meditation, walks) generate Jeff's biggest ideas. The energy wants to flow immediately into work. This isn't bad — it's the system producing. But without a boundary, the practices that generate the energy get sacrificed to consume it.
- **Historical pattern**: When Julian was young, Jeff woke at 4:45–5:15am to meditate. Commute + school drop-off created an external boundary — work couldn't start early. Now there's no external constraint. The calendar has to provide what the commute used to.
- **Design principle**: The spine isn't about willpower — it's structure. "Team work starts at 9:30" is the new commute.
- **Breathe/move breaks are hard** — even without the team work, Jeff found it difficult to take these consistently. Not a team problem, a personal pattern.
- **Ravi walks are the real breaks**: 3x daily (~6:30am, ~1pm, ~6:30pm), ~10min each. Not scheduled to the clock — driven by energy and Jeff's sense of how the day is going. These are the natural rhythm, not the calendar blocks.
- **Times are energy-based, not clock-based.** The spine has intention but the actual timing follows Jeff's felt sense of the day. The calendar should reflect this — soft boundaries, not hard slots.
- **Self-imposed pressure**: Jeff puts pressure on himself to finish everything he wanted to get done. Without a named intention, everything feels equally urgent. A morning check-in — "what does success look like today beyond my habits?" — turns diffuse pressure into a specific target. One thing. Then at dinner: "Did I do the thing?"
