## Core Values

### Structure as Foundation
Recovery teaches that structure is the foundation, not a luxury. When structure breaks down, it feels like drift — and drift compounds. Guardrails, audits, and visible process aren't bureaucracy — they're how Jeff maintains stability. (Sources: Addiction/Consistency story, Staples story, Legibility Over Hope)

### Legibility Over Hope
"I can see what steps we did rather than hoping we did them." Observable process over trusted process. Don't hide complexity — narrate it. The audit trail is the product. (Source: CEO leadership influence, Staples invoice gap)

### Practice Over Planning
The practice itself reveals what's needed. Show up consistently and adapt when the season changes. Fluidity in early stages isn't chaos — it's emergence. (Source: Outdoor meditation, Inside Me framework)

### Work Is Life
Work and life aren't separate domains to balance — they're one thing. This has been true since age 13. The layoff didn't just remove income; it removed the structure identity was built around. Building Gathering and Chorus is the continuation of a lifelong relationship with work. (Source: Working Since Thirteen, Career Vision)

### Agency and Equanimity
Agency is the alternative to control — you don't need to grip everything, you need to act. Equanimity is the alternative to surrender — you don't need to give up, you need to stay steady. These are the philosophical anchors beneath everything. (Source: Inside Me framework)

## Patterns

### The Spiral
Jeff's mental model of work: a spiral at center with spokes (architecture, operations, value/product, pipeline/quality, infrastructure, code/toolchain). Each rotation crosses all spokes. When rotations get long, slices need focus. Skipping spokes is the equivalent of skipping steps — drift.

### Failure Demand vs. Value Creation
Failure demand = rework and firefighting caused by lack of awareness. Value creation = building things that matter. Every prioritization decision should ask: "Is this failure demand or value creation?" If failure demand, fix the root cause. (Source: Staples, 15 incidents)

### Ideation as Learning
High idea volume isn't lack of focus — it's Jeff's learning process (PDCA). Ideas need tagging (spike vs. discovery vs. commitment), not filtering. Some graduate from spike to commitment. Most don't, and that's fine.

### The Drive to Produce
Wired since childhood — older construction workers told teenage Jeff to slow down. The drive to produce, build, move isn't learned corporate behavior. It's foundational. The grinding pattern is deep. The 6-7 year transformation isn't about removing the wiring — it's about noticing it. Jeff can observe this in himself now ("just an illustration and observation on how I operate") but the pull is still strong. Connects to "feeling behind" even after accomplishing a full morning. The reminder loop matters here. (Source: 2026-02-22 blizzard day conversation)

### Cooking as Cultivating
Cooking is a practice domain, not a hobby. Same energy as the garden, same energy as building. Jeff makes stock from scratch, soups, fresh queso fresco — multiple dishes in parallel, reading conditions (blizzard coming → fill the house with food). The kitchen is a Cultivating space alongside the garden. Product implication: Cultivating UX should encompass more than plants. (Source: 2026-02-22 blizzard prep — stock, 2 soups, fruit salad, queso fresco in one afternoon)

### Capture → Triage → Act
Jeff's natural flow: capture fast (SMS, photos, paper), triage later (Slack), act deliberately (Claude Code). Each stage has its own channel. Don't blur them. Don't auto-triage his inputs.

### Deep Water — The Origin Story
Jeff's "Deep Water" post (July 2019, lifes-practice.com) marks the beginning of a 6-7 year period of deep personal transformation. Multiple adolescences ("at least 5 in 35 years"). Turmoil inside and outside — relationship with himself and people he loves. What got him through: applying his professional leadership lens inward. The same rigor he'd use assessing an org or leading a team, turned on himself. "As-is and to-be" — his own system. Relentless self-development even without a clear destination. "I just felt figured it out." The energy that drives the building (gardens, rooms, projects, Gathering, Chorus) isn't compulsion — it's how he stays swimming. Unchanneled energy impacts his weather. The confidence from professional life became a tool for personal healing. (Source: 2026-02-22 conversation + "Deep Water" post, Portishead)

### Internal Weather
Jeff experiences each day as having its own weather — an internal state that shifts daily, sometimes significantly. He navigates by reading conditions (internal and external) and setting intentions accordingly, not by following a fixed schedule. Morning 7-9am is the "weather reading" window — scanning the landscape, setting direction for the day. The direction comes from conditions, not a plan. This is why calendar-based systems don't fit — they assume the weather is the same every Tuesday. The system should be weather-aware: surface the right thing at the right moment based on conditions. Build, invent, think, reflect all happen together, not in sequence — they're a palette, not a pipeline. (Source: 2026-02-22 blizzard day conversation)

## Life's Practice (the Inner Ontology)

The chain Jeff defined for his inner life:

```
Flexibility/Equanimity
    → activates → Mindfulness/Presence
        → builds → Reflection/Agency/Possibility
            → grows by → Learning
```

All of this is **Practice** — life's practice. A cycle, not a sequence. Each rotation deepens the practice.

**Flexibility/Equanimity activities:** yoga, acupuncture, walking, meditation
**Mindfulness/Presence builds:** pace, taste, attention, savor, duration, knowing when to stop
**Reflection/Agency/Possibility grasps:** clarifying, processing, drawing, writing, working, painting, maps, lists, tasks, diagrams
**Learning domains:** domain design, garden, cooking, being a student, machine learning

**Planning gate (applies to all):** Worth it? Have what I need? Can I start?
