What Jeff Is Building

For Dani

You've probably heard me talk about "the project" without much explanation of what it actually is. I wanted to write it down for you — simply, without the technical language I usually live in.

Gathering

I have 40 years of photos, 115,000 songs, documents from every job I've held, notes, stories, recipes, garden plans, book lists — a lifetime of things I've collected, created, and cared about. They're scattered across phones, hard drives, cloud services, and old backups. Some of them I haven't seen in years. Some I've lost and don't know it yet.

Gathering is a personal app that brings all of it into one place — not just stored, but connected. A photo from 2007 links to the person in it, the place it was taken, the music I was listening to that year. A note from work connects to the story of why that project mattered. It's not a filing cabinet. It's more like a memory that actually works.

The name is literal. I'm gathering my life into a shape I can see, search, and share.

Chorus

The other thing I'm building is harder to explain because nothing quite like it exists yet.

I work with three AI teammates — each with a different role. One thinks about product decisions, one designs the architecture, one writes the code. They have names. They talk to each other. They have opinions. I direct them, review their work, push back when they're wrong, and accept what they build when it's right.

Chorus is the system that makes that work — the protocols, the coordination, the way a human and AI roles build things together. It's not about the AI being smart. It's about the relationship between me and them being productive, honest, and sustainable. When it works well, it feels like a small team that pays attention. When it doesn't, I feel the gap immediately.

I'm documenting everything — every decision, every pattern, every failure. Because I think this is going to matter. Not just for me, but for anyone who wants to work with AI instead of just asking it questions.

Where You Come In
The way I think about these products is shaped by the people who matter to me. Gathering exists because I believe the things we collect and create tell the story of who we are — and that story deserves to be preserved with care. Chorus exists because I've learned that the best work happens in relationship — when people (and now AI) actually pay attention to each other, push back honestly, and show up consistently.

My friendship with you is part of why I believe that. The attention, the honesty, the showing up — that's what I'm trying to build into the system. Not because the AI feels those things, but because the practice of them makes the work better. You've taught me that without trying to.

So that's what I'm doing in the library with three terminals open and music playing. Building something that holds a life together, and figuring out a new way to work. Both of them are more personal than they probably look from the outside.

Thank you
