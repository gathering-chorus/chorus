## What It Is

Gathering is Jeff Bridwell's externalized mind — a garden of meaning built on semantic web principles.

It's a memory layer that sits across his entire digital life — books, photos, music, blog posts, properties, ideas, projects — and makes the connections between them visible, searchable, and meaningful. Like a garden, the value isn't in any single plant. It's in the ecosystem: how things grow together, what feeds what, what blooms in which season.

Content stays where it lives. Gathering owns the *memory* of what Jeff has, when it mattered, and how things connect. A book that influenced a garden plan. A photo from a trip that connects to a blog post. A song linked to a memory. Seeds from different sources, planted in the same soil, growing roots between them.

## Who It Serves

Jeff. One person. This is not a platform, not a product for others, not a social network. It's a personal tool built by and for someone who thinks in connections, carries a lot, and needs a system that thinks with him — not just stores for him.

Gathering also serves a broader purpose. Jeff has been out of work since August 2023 and is deliberately not returning to the same career shape (leading large engineering orgs). Gathering is four things at once:
1. **Growth investment** — professional and personal development through building with current technologies
2. **Career incubator** — exploring what a different shape of work looks like, with Gathering as potential seed
3. **Capability portfolio** — demonstrating not just technical skill but systems thinking, philosophical grounding, and the ability to see across domains
4. **Digital inheritance** — a record of how Jeff thought, what he connected, and why things mattered. Not a file dump — a map of a life lived deliberately. At the end, this is what remains: the gathered meaning, the tended connections, the annotated journey. The Feeling layer is what makes this an inheritance, not an archive.

This means the public-facing dimension matters. Not everything graduates — but what does should demonstrate how Jeff thinks, not just what he builds.

Three possible directions are already germinating:
- **Technology consulting practice** — semantic web, AI, privacy architecture, systems thinking
- **Urban gardening business** — Light Life Urban Gardens (from notebook), property domain already modeled
- **Content publishing** — professional and personal channels, leveraging the graduation model

All three are domains in the graph. The emergence lifecycle (ideas → projects → releases) isn't just a feature — it's how Jeff's next chapter takes shape. Gathering is the incubator.

## The Philosophical Frame

Jeff hasn't just read philosophy — he's built a coherent philosophical operating system that the product embodies:

- **Heidegger** — Gathering (Versammlung): meaning coheres through relations, not central control. A thing is defined by what it gathers, not its material. Dwelling: not storing but inhabiting. The knowledge graph is a place you dwell in.
- **Sartre** — Radical freedom, authenticity, bad faith. The graduation model is a choice. The system never pushes toward publishing — the gardener decides.
- **Buddhist psychology** — Agency (cetanā + sati + viriya), balance (equanimity under load), heart (hṛdaya: the seat of consciousness, not just emotion). Gather without reifying — the system stays workable because nothing in it is fixed.
- **Greek metaphysics** — Cosmos: meaningful order from chaos. The ontology IS the cosmos.
- **Memory science** — Memory as reconstruction, not storage. Consolidation, reconsolidation, schemas. The knowledge graph mirrors how memory actually works.
- **Cognitive psychology** — Reflectivity: the mind observing its own processes. Saturation: real processing limits. Double-loop learning: questioning assumptions, not just methods.
- **Sanskrit** — Hṛdaya: the heart-mind that gathers, holds, and radiates. The center of consciousness.

The axis of the product is **reactivity → reflectivity**: from raw experience driving behavior, to experience flowing through awareness, intention, and choice before becoming action. The Self domain makes this axis navigable.

## The Garden Frame

Gathering is a garden, not a factory. The metaphor isn't decorative — Jeff is a passionate gardener, the ontology already models gardens, and the lifecycle of a garden maps precisely to how the system works:

- **Seeds** — raw ideas, notebook scribbles, captured photos, L0 references. They enter the soil unformed. Not everything germinates.
- **Planting** — committing a seed to the graph. Giving it structure, a URI, a place in the ontology. The act of saying "this matters enough to tend."
- **Tending** — curation. Adding connections, annotations, personal meaning. L1 promoting to L2. The daily work of the knowledge graph. This is where the AI thinking partner helps — surfacing what needs attention.
- **Growth** — ideas developing into projects, connections multiplying, metadata enriching over time. Growth is not linear — it's seasonal, cyclical, sometimes dormant.
- **Harvest** — already the named pattern. Gathering metadata from external sources into the graph. But also: the moment when something grown in the garden is ready to be used, shared, or offered.
- **Fruiting** — readiness for graduation. The content has matured. It's ready to move from private to shared or public. The gardener decides when to pick — the system never forces ripeness.
- **Compost** — ideas that don't graduate aren't failures. They decompose and feed the soil. A parked idea informs a future project. A dismissed AI suggestion still enriched the context. Nothing is wasted.
- **Seasons** — the concentric circles pattern. Each pass through data → security → features → automation is a growing season. The garden looks different each cycle, richer each year.
- **Perennials** — long-running themes that return season after season (books, property, the blog). The root system is deep.
- **Annuals** — one-off projects, experiments, things that bloom once and feed the compost.

The garden is the private space. The market is the storefront. Content grows in the garden before it moves to the market — if it moves at all.

## The Encapsulation Frame

Gathering is a tool for healthy psychological encapsulation — containing the full richness of internal experience while exposing only what's intentional, values-aligned, and ready.

The two images define this precisely:

- **As-is (self-portrait) = reactivity.** Experience flows to reaction. The red zones at the foundation — confidence, desiring attention/understanding/love/support — drive behavior before reflection can intervene. Hedging/edging is the tension of trying to pause in a reactive system. Under-encapsulation: raw internal state leaking to output.
- **To-be (Miró fence) = reflectivity.** Experience flows through awareness, intention, and choice before becoming action. The gathered connections create the space to *see* before acting. Beauty is what reflection looks like. Healthy encapsulation: rich internal state, intentional external expression.

Gathering is the system that moves from one to the other. The knowledge graph is mindfulness middleware — it buffers between raw experience and action, surfaces what matters, and lets the gardener decide.

| Software encapsulation | Gathering | Buddhist analog |
|----------------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Private internal state | Everything inside the garden wall | Sense restraint — letting experience arise without leaking |
| Public interface | The market — graduated content | Right Speech — intentional, skillful expression |
| Controlled access | The gate — shared visibility | Non-identification — processes, not identity |
| Invariants enforced internally | Ontology, ACLs, the wall | Values — internal coherence under load |
| Implementation changes safely | L1 promotes to L2 without breaking the interface | Growth without identity collapse |

The spectrum of risk:
- **Over-encapsulation**: Everything stays private forever. The garden never opens. The portfolio purpose fails. Psychological analog: suppression, rigidity, inaccessibility.
- **Under-encapsulation**: No privacy model. Raw data leaks to public. The broken middleware before Kade fixed it. Psychological analog: reactivity, emotional flooding, boundary violations.
- **Healthy encapsulation**: Capture everything (feel fully). Surface connections (observe clearly). Graduate when ready (respond intentionally). The graduation model.

## Six Feature Domains (from the self-portrait outputs)

### 1. Collecting — The Harvest
Gather metadata from external sources into the graph. L1 catalog for most, L2 where personal meaning matters, L3 for authored content. Each harvest brings new seeds into the garden — some will be tended into rich connections, most will remain cataloged and searchable.

Shipped: Apple Music (66,444 tracks, 5,844 albums, 7,327 artists), Apple Photos (7,978 items with GPS + face detection), WordPress (40+ posts), Books (photo → AI → pod pipeline). Remaining: Google Photos (1M+), Spotify, Facebook, LinkedIn, local media (200TB).

### 2. Connecting — The Root System
Cross-domain relationships via RDF ontology. The graph sees what no single service can. Like roots beneath the soil, connections are invisible from the surface but they're what holds the garden together. Measured by cross-domain connection ratio — if this drops as sources are added, the garden is accumulating topsoil without deepening roots.

### 3. Integrating — The Soil
The semantic memory layer. SOLID pods as source of truth, Fuseki as query engine, ontology as shared vocabulary. The soil holds everything — nutrients, structure, memory. Privacy-first graduation model: Private → Shared → Public. The garden wall protects what's growing.

### 4. Feeling — The Tending
Personal annotations, ratings, "when it mattered." The L2 enrichment layer. Not just metadata — memory. The gardener's knowledge of what each plant needs, which ones are thriving, which are struggling. This is what makes it an extended mind instead of a catalog — the care invested over time.

### 5. Doing — The Lifecycle
Seeds → seedlings → growth → fruiting → harvest → compost. In system terms: capture → idea → project → release → archive. Some things are perennials that return every season. Some are annuals that bloom once. Some seeds never germinate — they feed the compost. The lifecycle is cyclical, not linear. Nothing is wasted. SMS capture (Twilio + Whisper for voice/video) is the primary intake channel — Jeff's natural pattern is texting himself, then triaging later.

### 6. Knowing — The Self Domain
The integrating domain that gives all others meaning. Not metadata — first-class navigable content. Jeff has assembled a coherent philosophical operating system: Heidegger (dwelling, Versammlung), Sartre (radical freedom, authenticity), Buddhist psychology (agency, balance, equanimity, heart/hṛdaya), Greek metaphysics (cosmos as meaningful order), modern neuroscience (memory as reconstruction), and cognitive psychology (reflectivity, saturation, double-loop learning). This is the domain where the gardener is himself a plant — observable, tended, growing. The axis of the product (reactivity → reflectivity) lives here.

### Plus: The Thinking Partner — The Gardener's Companion
Conversational AI that sees the garden whole, notices what's ready, what's struggling, what could connect. Surfaces patterns the gardener might miss: "this seedling and that perennial share the same light needs." Not a chatbot. Not a search engine. The answer to "no teacher" — a companion that helps the gardener see the garden he's already growing. Architecturally necessary for tending at scale.

## The Privacy Architecture — The Garden Wall

Everything starts inside the garden wall. Graduation is always the gardener's choice.

- **Private/Hidden** (inside the wall): Only Jeff. The growing space. Seeds, experiments, half-formed ideas, personal memories. Default for everything.
- **Shared** (the gate): Invited guests see specific beds. Read or read+write grants to specific people/groups per collection. Showing a trusted friend what's growing.
- **Public** (the market): Open web. `foaf:Agent` in SOLID terms. No login required. What Jeff chooses to offer the world — the fruit, not the process.

The system never pushes toward the market. The wall stays up. The gardener opens the gate.

## Honest Assessment

**The ambition is coherent, not scattered.** Everything connects back to one idea: externalize what Jeff carries, make the connections visible, graduate what's ready. That's not scope creep — it's a unified vision with a lot of surface area. One garden, many beds.

**The foundation is real — and now it's populated.** Three harvest seasons complete (WordPress → Music → Photos). 80,000+ items in the graph. 147 API endpoints documented. 19 views under a unified style guide. The soil is rich and the beds are full.

**The Self domain is the integrating move.** 17 philosophical/psychological/personal frameworks captured — this is the domain that gives all other domains meaning. It's also the most unusual and differentiating part of the product. The risk: treating it as another collection (a list of frameworks) instead of what it is (a navigable web of how Jeff thinks). The UX has to earn this distinction.

**The risk has shifted from foundation to expression.** The technical platform works. The question now is: what does this look like to the world? Public storefronts, the thinking partner, the Self domain UX — these are all expression problems, not infrastructure problems. The garden is full; it needs paths, gates, and a good view from the road.

## Current Season

The foundation is built. Music, Photos, Books, Property, Blog, SMS/Capture, Glimmer, and Ideas are all live. The garden wall holds (ACL + visibility middleware complete). Three seasons of harvest (WordPress → Music → Photos) proved the repeatable pattern works.

**This season's focus**:
1. **Self domain** — the integrating domain. 17 philosophical/psychological/personal frameworks captured. Needs ontology modeling (RDF classes and properties) and UX direction ("contemplative space, not a form").
2. **UX polish** — the garden is full but the paths between beds need work. Style guide shipped, but navigation, rough edges, and visual quality remain.
3. **Thinking partner** — the `/chat` route that makes the graph conversational. The missing companion.
4. **Public face** — storefronts, graduation, what the world sees.
