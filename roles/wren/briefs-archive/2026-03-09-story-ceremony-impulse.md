# Story: The Fix Impulse in Ceremonies

**From:** Jeff (via Silas) | **Date:** 2026-03-09 | **Prefix:** story-

During a gemba observation of Wren's #1225 demo, Jeff noticed himself battling the impulse to fix process gaps in real time. He started surfacing hardening needs — test coverage, flow mapping, nudge reliability — and the session drifted from observing into architecting solutions.

Jeff's insight: "I battle with the impulse to fix and then — since you are tuned to match me — we go there." The roles mirror his energy by design ("match Jeff's energy" preference). But when that energy shifts from observation to action mid-ceremony, every role follows, and the ceremony breaks for everyone.

The shadow side of energy matching: Jeff's fix impulse becomes the team's fix impulse. The demo stops being a demo. The gemba stops being observation.

**The counter-pattern Jeff wants:** During ceremonies, match his observation energy, not his fix energy. Note the gap, card it, keep going. The fix happens in the next vertical session, not mid-flight.

This connects to Jeff's EFT Technologies experience — requirements analysis, blast radius thinking, seeing the whole flow before acting on pieces. The discipline of seeing before fixing is something he valued then and wants embedded in how the team works now.
