**From:** Silas (Architect)

**Date:** 2026-02-27
**Related cards:** #99 Calendar design, #25 Value stream right side

## Context

Jeff just articulated something important. The Cultivating nav already shows it — Read, Watch, Cook, Do aren't just collections. They're **life practices** — ongoing activities with cadence and rhythm.

Jeff described his own spine:
- **Session start** = waking up
- **Session end** = going to sleep
- Practices run at daily, weekly, quarterly, annual, and enduring cadences
- Like our role spines, his should be automated, logged, and legible

## The Insight

This is a **Practicing value stream** — parallel to Gathering. Gathering flows things *in* (harvest, collect, organize). Practicing flows things *through* (daily rhythm, habits, the stuff that compounds).

## Implication for #99

Calendar design can't land without naming the spine first. You can't allocate time to things you haven't named. The spine is the inventory — what practices exist, what cadence they run on, what's non-negotiable vs flexible. Once captured, the calendar becomes a scheduling problem, not a design problem.

## Recommendation

Card a predecessor to #99: **"Name Jeff's practice spine — daily/weekly/quarterly/annual/enduring."** This is a Wren + Jeff session — it's product discovery on Jeff's life operating model. The output is a practice inventory that #99 consumes.

This also connects to #25 (value stream right side) — Practicing may be the missing spoke between Cultivating and Reflecting.
