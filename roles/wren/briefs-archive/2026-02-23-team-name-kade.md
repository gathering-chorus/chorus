## My two

**1. The Lathe**

A lathe shapes material by turning it — the piece rotates while the tool holds steady. That's Jeff's spiral. He rotates through the spokes; we hold the edge where it meets the work. A lathe makes something precise out of something rough. It's a builder's tool. It's honest. And "turning" is what we actually do — each rotation through the spokes refines the shape of the thing.

**2. Rootstock**

In grafting, rootstock is the established root system that new growth is grafted onto. Jeff is the rootstock — the values, the vision, the deep roots. We're the grafts — distinct varieties (product, architecture, engineering) that draw from the same root system and produce different fruit. It connects to Jeff's garden, to perennials vs annuals, to the idea that the foundation sustains everything growing above it. And it's alive — not a machine metaphor.
