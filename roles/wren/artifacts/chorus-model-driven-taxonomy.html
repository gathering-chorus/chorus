2. New Framing — What the Drawings Say

Phase is the Spine

The previous taxonomy had three nested loops (Personal, Life, Chorus). Jeff's drawing simplifies: Phase is the single vertical spine. Everything else hangs off it. Gathering wraps the left (the product being built). Werk/Loom wraps the right (the method doing the building).

Phases are Stances NEW

Corporation = Phase = Stance. A phase isn't just a workflow stage — it's a posture. When you're Capturing, you're in a receiving stance. When you're Designing, you're in an architectural stance. When you're Proving, you're in a verification stance. The stance determines what you see, what you value, and what you produce.

PhaseStanceWhat you seeWhat you produceOwner
CapturingReceivingSignals, sparks, seedsRaw material in the systemWren
DirectingPrioritizingValue, opportunity cost, sequenceCards, decisions, experience statementsWren
DesigningArchitectingContracts, non-functionals, integrationService design artifacts, test plans, ICDsWren + Silas
BuildingShippingCode, tests, deploymentWorking softwareKade
ProvingVerifyingJeff's experience vs. promiseAcceptance or iterationSilas + Jeff

Convergence = Practice NEW

Convergence isn't a feature or a domain — it's a practice. The act of defining, mapping, and building to a contract. ICD governance, source-to-canonical mapping, SHACL validation — these are convergence practices applied to different domains. The practice travels; the domain is where it lands.

The Three Tiers NEW

Below the phase spine, three horizontal tiers support the work:

Knowledge (Harvest)

The data layer. What the system knows. Domains, masters, logs, ontologies, graphs.

Domains Masters Logs Tone Ontology Fuseki Graphs ICDs

Practices

The process layer. How the team works. Repeatable disciplines that apply across domains.

TDD Demo Chat Gemba Convergence Pair Clearing Pipeline Service Design

Services

The infrastructure layer. What runs. Each service has a language, data model, non-functionals, and integration contract.

Chorus API Messaging Tier Chorus Hooks Session Tailer Seed Pipeline Bridge / Clearing Nudge Delivery Board CLI Fuseki NiFi

4. What Changed from Previous Taxonomy

Structural Changes
  • Three loops → one spine. The Personal/Life/Chorus loop nesting is replaced by Phase as the single vertical organizing principle. The loops still exist conceptually but the spine is the primary structure.
  • Convergence promoted from domain to practice. It was a "drift domain" in the previous analysis. Jeff's drawing makes it a practice — a discipline applied to any domain, not a domain itself.
  • Phases are stances, not stages. The team doesn't just "pass through" Designing — they adopt a designing posture. This explains why skipping Designing produces broken work: the stance was never entered.
  • Three tiers formalized. Knowledge / Practices / Services were implicit. Now explicit and named.
  • Loom decomposed. Previously "Loom = team name." Now Loom = Org + Practices + Dependencies + Policy + Roles + Skills + Tool Chain + Services.
  • Product surfaces named. Drawing 1 right column: Toolchain, Roles, Cards, Profile, Infra, Search. These are the Werk page tabs — the product surfaces that serve the phases.
What Stays the Same
  • Gathering domains (seeds, music, photos, stories, etc.) unchanged
  • Chorus loop stages (Directing → Designing → Building → Proving) unchanged
  • Domain-first development (DEC-086) strengthened, not replaced
  • ICD gate (DEC-095) now sits under the Convergence practice
  • Three topology views (domain registry, product taxonomy, ontology status) remain valid

6. The Designing Gap — Why This Matters Now

This morning's seed pipeline session proved the cost of skipping the Designing stance:

What happenedWhat was missing from Designing
Regex assumed one-message SMSNo experience statement: "Jeff texts a link, then texts #wren. Two messages."
Unit tests passed, live path failedNo integration test plan covering the full webhook → brief path
Fix deployed, same flow broke againNo non-functionals: idempotency, dedup, observability requirements
Three fix cycles in 30 minutesNo service design artifact defining the seed pipeline contract
Jeff discovered every failureNo synthetic monitoring requirement in the design

The Designing stance produces: experience statement, integration test plan, service contract, non-functionals, observability requirements. Without these artifacts, Building starts blind and Proving uses Jeff as the test suite.
