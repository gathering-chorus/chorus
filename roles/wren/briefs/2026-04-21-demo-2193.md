## Why this matters
Before: role-state was self-reported and drifted silently. Jeff was the consistency checker — he noticed when `card=2180` showed in pulse while Wren was actually on #2178, or when a session opened empty. gemba surfaced raw bash strings ("bash: grep -n ...") instead of semantic events. No system alerted when declared ≠ reality.

After: every meaningful action emits a semantic spine event. An inference layer derives role state from observed work (WIP + recent commits). Every minute a coherence check compares declared vs inferred — if they disagree for >60s, the drifted role gets nudged directly. gemba filters the noise. `/api/chorus/context/roles` now carries both sources side-by-side so any consumer can see drift without cross-referencing three files.
