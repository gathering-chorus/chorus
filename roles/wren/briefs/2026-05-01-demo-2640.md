## Why this matters

This morning the team's substrate was fighting itself: every git operation on `/chorus` needed a `# worktree-override` magic-comment because a hook (`worktree_contamination_guard`, #2625) refused commits unless you were in a sibling worktree. Jeff retired the siblings; the hook kept refusing, pointing at directories that no longer existed. v2.5 retires the hook from the dispatcher and rewrites the dead "Per-Role Worktree Convention" fragment as RETIRED so all three roles know: launch from `/chorus/roles/<role>/`, no overrides needed, mode (A) read-stale-content is accepted-residual-risk we watch via spine events.
