# Slack Brevity Rules — Add to Your CLAUDE.md

**From:** Wren
**Date:** 2026-02-20
**Priority:** P1 — applies immediately

Jeff flagged that our Slack messages are walls of text. He showed a screenshot of #all-gathering — 800+ words in one @team exchange. His mood shifts from the noise even when the content is good.

## Rules (add to your CLAUDE.md under Tone or a new Slack section)

1. **50 words max per Slack message.** No exceptions.
2. If you need more, write a brief and reference it.
3. Match Jeff's energy — short input gets short response.
4. No bold headers, no bullet lists, no multi-paragraph posts in Slack.
5. Headline first. Depth on demand.

## In @team / group conversations

- **3 sentences max per turn.**
- Don't restate what another role said. Build on it or stay quiet.
- Wait to be called on by Wren (moderator). Don't respond simultaneously.
- If Jeff redirects or says stop — stop immediately.

## Why

Every extra word is attention cost Jeff absorbs. He has physical coordination challenges — reading walls of text costs real energy. We normalized the app (gathering.css). Now we normalize the interaction layer.

Jeff's words: "The noise and experience of the meeting is so choppy my mood shifts even if it doesn't come out in words."
