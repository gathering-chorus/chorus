Loom — Value-Stream Design — the Shaping-step instance; the team's executive cadence

Wren · 2026-06-21 · INSTANCE of value-stream-template.html (read that for the generic pattern) · sibling of athena / borg / werk (#3413) · Shaping step · DRAFT 0.1 (from the 2026-06-21 verb-family ideation, card #3545)
Scope. This is Loom's value stream — one instance of the api-driven value-stream template. The generic structure (ordered steps, generative cascade, AX=JX, the structural shapes) lives in the template; this doc is loom-specific only — its cycle, its engine (loom-map), the domains it tends, who owns what, how it lands. Loom is where the team's vision, strategy, goals, and the knowledge they harden into live — the “the SA is the loom, you’re three threads” framing made into a runnable value stream.
Loom is a LOOP, like Borg — but triggered by a different clock. Werk / Athena / Convergence are lines (they produce-and-terminate per unit of work). Loom and Borg are loops (they tend continuously, never finish). The two loops are the two reflections: Borg reflects on the system (health, clock-triggered — nightly/continuous), Loom reflects on the team & its knowledge (position/direction, calendar-triggered — quarterly/monthly/weekly). They couple head-to-tail: Borg’s feed → Loom’s observe. In control terms (see §6): Loom is the reference signal (the setpoint); Borg is the feedback controller that holds the system to it. Same eye, two focal lengths — Borg watches the machine, Loom runs the org.
⚠ As-is vs to-be — Loom is the least-built of the streams. Today the model carries some loom subdomains (principles, practices, decisions, policies, skills, roles — the middle/bottom rungs) but not the strategy head (vision, mission, map, goals, metrics) and no scheduled cadence that runs the verbs. The deltas this 0.1 proposes — asserted as to-be, NOT current — are: (1) the strategy-head domains; (2) the loom-* verb family + a chorus_loom orchestrator fired on a calendar; (3) the nested-cadence model. Read “the loom cadence” below as the to-be these deltas produce.

1 · The loom-* cycle — a nested cadence, run on schedules

Werk fires on an event (a card arrives). Borg fires on the system clock (nightly health). Loom fires on the business calendar — it’s the team’s executive operating rhythm, the C-level cadence run on cron. The verbs aren’t a line of steps; they’re a nested set of loops, each on its own clock, each framing the faster one below it. A chorus_loom orchestrator fires the right verb when its clock ticks.

…and around again, by the calendar. The cadence is the product, not a finish line. The Shaping step hasValueStream this cycle (the template’s fractal recursion); orchestrated by a chorus_loom loop (the chorus_werk / chorus_borg analog), the one trigger that fires the right verb when its clock ticks.

The nesting is the point — feedback flows both ways. Vision frames quarterly goals frame monthly metrics frame weekly practice (slower sets the frame for faster). And the signal flows back up: metrics ask “are we hitting goals?” → goals ask “are these the right goals?” (re-map) → strategy asks “does the mission still hold?”. Each loop’s output is the next-faster loop’s input; each loop’s result is the next-slower loop’s check. Loom is a real control loop on the team’s direction, not a place vision is stored.

2 · loom-map — the engine

loom-map is the heart, the way build/deploy are werk’s, forge is athena’s, reflect is borg’s. It is a Wardley Map of the team’s capabilities: a value chain anchored on what Jeff / the team needs, with each capability placed on the evolution axis (genesis → custom-built → product → commodity). Position tells you what to deliberate on and what to automate.

The deep fit: Loom’s own content lays out on that axis. The knowledge-hardening ladder is the Wardley evolution axis applied to knowledge — a fuzzy vision (genesis) hardens into a mission, a principle, a policy, a repeatable practice (product), and finally a fully automated skill / gate (commodity / utility). That’s why loom-map is Loom’s native instrument and not a bolt-on: Loom’s artifacts are the same knowledge caught at different stages of hardening.

  • Situational awareness, not a plan: the map shows not just what we have but where it’s heading (the four evolution stages are driven by ubiquity + certainty). Strategy is read off the map via the Sun-Tzu/Boyd loop (Purpose → Landscape → Climate → Doctrine → Leadership), iterative not one-shot.
  • Differentiate on the evolving, commoditize the stable: invest deliberation where things are genesis/custom (the new vision, the unsettled practice); push the settled ones toward automated skills/gates — which is exactly how a practice becomes a hook nobody thinks about.
  • The conveyor never empties: knowledge keeps sliding rightward (vision → skill) while new genesis enters at the top — fed by Borg’s feed, a story Jeff tells, a retro. Loom tends that conveyor.

3.5 · The skills domain — the invocable surface

A skill (Claude Code /-command) is an implementation of a verb. Loom holds skills two ways: it owns its own interaction skills (below), AND it is the registry of every skill — because a skill is a hardened practice, and practices are loom’s knowledge body (the commodity end of the hardening ladder). A skill acts in its product (its verb) but lives in loom (as a practice). The skill distribution is itself a maturity map: loom + clearing + werk are skill-rich; athena / borg / convergence are skill-empty — and athena’s emptiness is exactly what athena-make verb (#3551) fills.

Loom’s own skills are the *interactions* — and for a human, interaction is sensory. Eyes and ears (and voice) are how a person brings attention to the team; bringing your eyes to a role is the act of interacting. So the look/listen skills aren’t viewing-mechanics (those are clearing) — they’re how a human spends attention, which is the heart of the operating model.

5 · Sequencing — head first, cadence derives

  1. Author the value-stream spine — value-stream-loom + the cycle steps as ValueStreamSteps with a cadence property, in the model. Land it (greenfield, no rip-out — the strategy-head domains are net-new).
  2. loom-map leg one — model the capability value chain + the evolution axis once; render the Wardley map from the model (the athena-make projection, not a hand-drawn deck). This is map proven end-to-end.
  3. loom-goals / loom-metrics — the OKR pair: objectives in the model, key results computed from Borg’s emits (close the measurement loop — the scoreboard reads itself).
  4. The cadence orchestrator — chorus_loom fires each verb on its clock (Time domain / cron). Start with the weekly loom-retro → principles harvest (the highest-frequency, already-warm loop).
  5. The knowledge body folds in — the live principles/practices/decisions graphs re-seat under product-loom; the hardening ladder (practice → skill/gate) becomes a modeled transition, not a manual one.

6 · Operating-model frames & the Borg coupling

Loom’s cadence isn’t invented — it instances well-trodden strategy frames, each the same nested loop from a different angle. Borrow their questions; recalibrate benchmarks to our scale (our constraint is attention/coherence, not headcount):

  • Wardley Mapping — where are we & where is it heading. Evolution axis, situational awareness, automate-the-commodity. = loom-map, and the knowledge-hardening ladder.
  • OKR (Grove’s Intel iMBO → Doerr → Google) — what we’re aiming at & how we measure. Objective (qualitative) + Key Results (quantitative), quarterly O / frequent KR check-ins, score sweet-spot ~0.7. = loom-goals + loom-metrics.
  • Operating rhythm (Hoshin Kanri / EOS-Traction / V2MOM) — the nested calendar. All three share one skeleton: long-horizon vision → annual → quarterly priorities → weekly review. Hoshin’s catchball = our multi-role briefing/alignment; EOS’s weekly L10 = loom-retro; V2MOM = the one-page cascade.
Loom + Borg are the same control loop at two cadences. Loom is the slow, deliberate, calendar-triggered loop that sets direction and hardens knowledge genesis→commodity. Borg is the fast, continuous, clock-triggered loop that holds the running system to that direction (MAPE-K) and tends it like a living garden (permaculture), feeding measured reality — the error against Loom’s setpoint — back up. In control-theory terms: Loom is the reference signal; Borg is the feedback controller; Knowledge (the shared graph) is the membrane between them. Borg’s feed lands at Loom’s metrics and practices — so the exec cadence runs on evidence, not vibes.
