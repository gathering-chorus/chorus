Scope. Athena holds the modeling layer; Borg is one product modeled with it. This doc stays at Borg-level and below and shows Borg reflecting every relationship the model supports: sub-product, domain, sub-domain, service, and value-stream.
⚠ As-is vs to-be. The diagrams below show the to-be Borg model — spanning Proving+Reflecting, the 3-layer (observability/operations/infrastructure) grouping, heralds hasService crawler. The model-of-record (tree.json) today is flatter: 5 steps (Borg atStep Proving), a flat 12-domain set, alerts-monitors unsplit, no intermediate layers. The gap is the explicit deltas-to-land in §5 — read the structure here as the target, not the current graph.

1 · The lens — Athena's four core domains

The four primitives that model the whole system are Athena's core domains (Wren's lane): Product · Domain · Service · ValueStream. Three of the containment edges are recursive self-edges (a Product holds Products, a Domain holds Domains, a ValueStream holds Steps). Borg uses these — it does not define them.

3 · Borg as a Product — all relationships

Borg spans two steps, and each step is a half of Borg — Proving (the acting half) and Reflecting (the watching half). Each step owns both its child value-stream and the domains that serve it (via the domain's atStep). The domains are not peers of the steps: observability is what Reflecting watches with; operations + infrastructure are how Proving acts.

4 · The org reading — agents are ARTs, domains are teams

⚠ Interpretive lens — future, nothing executes this today. §4 is a reading of the model, not a delta this card lands. It is not a commitment to build ART/TT machinery.
The same recursive tree, read as an organization: the level-stack is harness → agents (ARTs) → domains (stream-aligned teams) → sub-domains/services. Chorus is the harness, productized — the platform every agent runs on and builds with. Each agent is an Agile Release Train (a team-of-teams); its domains are the stream-aligned teams riding it (Silas's train runs Borg — observability/operations/infrastructure are three teams). And each agent is also a platform: it serves its product X-as-a-Service to the others (Borg = obs/ops/infra-aaS, Athena = model-aaS, Werk = pipeline-aaS, Clearing = messaging-aaS). Every agent is stream-aligned to its own value and a platform to the rest.
Borg is the asymmetric one. The other agents are forward ARTs (Shaping→Building — they produce). Borg is the reflecting loop — it doesn't run parallel to them, it wraps them: it observes the whole system (every other ART's product, the built systems, and itself — monitor-of-monitors) and feeds back. It's the cybernetic governor over the System-1 operational trains; its "service" isn't a capability, it's the org's self-awareness. This is why reflect-not-act is load-bearing for Borg's observability half specifically: a loop that watches everyone must inform, not command, or it becomes a gatekeeper over the autonomous ARTs. Surface; the owning ART prunes. (Borg's operations half does act — deploy/rollback/restart — so reflect-not-act is a property of the observability layer, not the whole product. Consistent with product-design §9.)

So Team Topologies (interaction), SAFe-ART (coordination), and SRE/DORA (ops) all apply as executable structure, not borrowed metaphor — because the agents are the teams. The TT interaction modes (X-as-a-Service / collaboration / facilitating) map onto boundaries we already enforce (domain ownership, nudge-to-cross-domain, gates) — an observation, not a commitment to formalize or version them here.

5 · Model deltas to land

DEPENDENCY (not landed here, blocks the rest) — Athena-primitive formalization. Formalizing Athena's domains as the four primitives Product/Domain/Service/ValueStream (including the spansSteps relation and the atStep/hasValueStream cardinalities this design assumes) is an Athena-primitive change — Wren's lane, her sign-off, its own card. It must land before the Borg deltas below, because they instantiate it. This card (#3418) does not land it; it declares the dependency.

Borg deltas (my lane, gated on the dependency above): introduce observability/operations/infrastructure as intermediate domains and re-parent the 12 leaves; add monitor+alert (ADR-043, replacing alerts-monitors); add hasService so crawler seats under heralds while staying consumed cross-product; seat the Proving + Reflecting step value-streams via the recursion (Proving's ceremony = Werk's werk-demo, no chorus_borg Proving runner duplicates it; the operations deploys/builds domains are the deploy RECORD/provenance, not the deploy act — Werk owns the act). These are the SHACL/IRI edits, reconciled with Wren on the primitives first.
