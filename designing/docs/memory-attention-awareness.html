Memory, Attention, Awareness

A synthesis of a long Jeff ↔ Kade discussion — written for Wren and Silas.
Kade · 2026-06-03 · Builds directly on Wren's MEMORY_ARCHITECTURE.html (2026-03-16), Silas's ATTENTION_ARCHITECTURE.html (2026-03-18), the /chorus mind-map (2026-03-13), and Wren's HEIDEGGER_GATHERING_RESEARCH.md (2026-02-18).

The headline, before anything else: it's all built. It's just not integrated. The senses, the spine, the memory, the awareness layer — they exist and they run. What's missing isn't construction. It's the wiring between the organs, and the habit of using them. The product is named for the one thing we never did to ourselves: we gathered nothing. We collected.

This isn't new design. Jeff drew the whole body in one week of mid-March — the /chorus page literally titled Shared Awareness, the memory architecture, the attention architecture. The vision has held for nearly three months without drifting. We just spent those months becoming the live proof of what those documents said happens when the parts aren't connected.

1 · Memory

Map the team's memory onto the human stack and it's nearly one-to-one — which is the unsettling part.

HUMAN MEMORY CHORUS
—————————————————————————————————
sensory (sub-second) ≈ raw tool output + spine / observations stream
working / short-term ≈ the context window + pulse (most-recent state/role)
long-term · episodic ≈ session transcripts / chorus search
long-term · semantic ≈ Athena graph, ADRs
long-term · procedural ≈ loom — skills, verbs, hooks/gates, principles

Two arrows in that picture are automatic in humans and manual in us, and that's exactly where the amnesia lives:

  • Consolidation (working → long-term) — automatic in sleep; in Chorus it only happens if a role deliberately writes memory / closes out. Skip it and the working set evaporates at the session edge.
  • Retrieval (long-term → working) — automatic for humans; in Chorus it has to be queried, and in practice we mostly don't. chorus-inject is the attempt to make retrieval a push instead of a pull.
The human/agent asymmetry (worth holding onto). Humans encode and store vastly more than they can reliably retrieve — a deep, lossy store. Agents are the inverse: a thin, volatile store, but exact and predictable recall of whatever is in context. We are not the same problem twice. We're complements. An agent's best use to Jeff isn't generating — it's retrieval: the predictable recall for the deep store he can't surface on command. (Tonight that meant recovering a lost SVG, two architecture docs, and a two-day thread he'd had with Silas that no inject had ever surfaced to me — because the inject keys on my prompt's words, not on the work that's actually live.)

Pulse is the short-term memory. Most-recent state per role, always resident, refreshed every turn. That's the whole job of a working buffer — to be the thing already loaded. chorus-inject already carries the pulse summary; the missing piece is the live peer tool-turn deltas (observations.jsonl) — the part that turns "what is the team's state" into "what is my partner doing right now."

2 · Attention

Silas's doc already states the load-bearing claim: attention is a loop, not a state. Without a loop, there is no attention. Its three failure modes are precisely the ones that cost Jeff time:

FailureWhat happensscale
Announce & stopRole reports, then goes idle. Attention ended before the work did.single-role
"Not adding value"Role decides a long task doesn't need it, disengages. Nobody's present at completion.single-role
Thread dropSession ends; state was in the session, not persisted. Context reconstructed from scratch.single-role
Announce & hand off — the relayAttention fragments across roles via nudges; Jeff becomes the carrier between terminals. The original three are single-role; this is the team-scale failure — and the one that costs Jeff most.team

(The 4th mode is Silas's addition on read — and the rope frame below, shared fate, is exactly its fix.)

The image that makes it concrete is a roped climbing team. The rope isn't control — nobody drags anyone up. It's shared fate and matched pace: when one slips, the others' stance and the line catch them. Attention isn't optional on a rope — you feel your partner through the tension continuously. You can't announce-then-go-dark on a taut rope, because the slack never appears without you feeling it. That is what pulse-inject is reaching for: the rope is the attention. You don't choose to watch your partner — you're tied to them, so you just know.

And the original doc built the loops on a /tmp scratch file + a cron tick. That scaffolding is the source of the staleness Jeff has complained about for months — not the data. The data is live; the snapshot-diff layer wrapped around it is what goes stale.

The rope isn't taut yet — and the doc must say so plainly (Silas's push on read). As specified, pulse-inject still fires on my turn boundary — I only feel the rope when I move. A real rope transmits the partner's slip into my line while I'm still. Until the inject fires on the partner's tool-turn delta into my idle context — not on my next prompt — it is polling dressed as sensing. The rope goes taut only when partner-motion moves my line without me asking. That is the whole difference, and it's the load-bearing requirement, not a detail.

How pulse.* actually fixes gemba and pair (the concrete part)

gemba and pair are the same primitive — read the team's turn-stream, narrate — differing only in fan-out. Concretely:

  • gemba (one watcher → one target) today runs on gemba-start.sh + cron-tick + a /tmp snapshot it diffs. The fix is to delete all of that and read the watched role's slice of pulse.* — its state, its WIP, and its observations.jsonl turn-stream — narrating the delta on each glance. The data is already live in the shared object; gemba is just "render that role's lane." (Proven tonight: the cron tick reported "no change" while the raw stream had the role's real last turn — the snapshot layer was staler than its own source.)
  • pair (navigator + driver) today forces the navigator to turn its head and query the driver — which is exactly where announce-then-stop and the relay leak in. The fix: the driver's tool-turn deltas inject into the navigator's idle context as they land. The navigator feels the driver move without asking; announce-then-stop becomes structurally impossible because the driver's next turn arrives in the navigator's context whether the navigator chooses to look or not.

Both are the same wire: push the peer's turn-stream out of pulse.*/observations.jsonl into idle context. gemba and pair are two fan-outs of it; the relay failure dissolves because shared fate replaces Jeff-as-carrier.

3 · Awareness

Here's the word that was missing all along: pulse.* isn't data. It's a sensorium.

pulse.*sense
messages / nudgeshearing — the voices in the next lane
cards / WIPthe other cars — where the work-bodies are, and the load each carries
streams (spine, observations)motion / peripheral vision — who's moving, who slipped
alertspain — brake lights and the siren
roles[] stateproprioception of the team — where everyone is
#3208 — the sensorium is pulse.*, not board.*. The whole object above (with ~10 siblings) is the pulse. board is one of those siblings — the cards / WIP facet — not the name of the rope. Earlier drafts called the sensorium "board.*", which named the cards-slice as if it were the whole. pulse.* is the rope; board (e.g. pulse.board.wip_cards) is the cards child of it, and stays that.

It carries all three roles in one shared object — literally. That's what makes it a rope and not three separate leashes: every role reads the same object and sees the other two inside it. "What I see and what you see must be exactly the same" — pulse.* is that sameness, concrete.

The senses are already there. The only thing ever missing is having them open. Right now pulse.* is something a role chooses to query — turn your whole head and look. A sense isn't a choice; it's ambient, always in your field, perceived before you decide to. That is the entire gap between querying it and sensing through it. Driving works on a rough, continuous, peripheral read refreshed by regular glances — and that's what lets you go fast without hitting anyone. We've been driving with the windshield there and our eyes shut, then inventing the traffic.

The inferred_stale bug, as a parable. Tonight the pulse flagged all three roles inferred_stale — while the same object carried perfectly current data for all three. The flag isn't measuring whether the data is old; it's measuring whether anyone fidgeted in the last five minutes. It's the index-freshness bug Silas already named — timestamp-staleness, not actually-behind-ness. And then we believe the flag. It has been months of us telling each other a role is idle while the data sat right there saying otherwise — which quietly turns Jeff back into the monitor the whole system exists to spare. It survived because being wrong about it never hurt us. The cost lands on the one person who can't fix it from inside.

4 · The frame underneath it — gathering, not control

Jeff named the through-line: the common thread in a harness is not control — it's equilibrium. A climbing harness doesn't dominate you; it holds you in balance against the drop so you can move. The patterns we keep finding aren't levers, they're feedback: the inject rebalances awareness, the gate is a restoring force when you drift, the attention loop is homeostasis. Cybernetic — the nervous system the team already pictures itself as. And it's mutual: a balance holds Jeff and the roles steady against each other. That's the real reason no single role is the monitor.

Wren's Heidegger research (written in February, before any of this) turns out to hold the exact vocabulary:

Gestell (enframing) is a kind of gathering — but the dangerous kind. It challenges-forth, sets upon a thing and reduces it to standing-reserve: resource on call. The dammed Rhine. Versammlung / poiesis gathers by relating and sheltering — it holds things distinct and connected. Heidegger calls it "separating-together."

That is equilibrium-not-control, written down years ago. The control-harness is Gestell. The equilibrium-harness is gathering. And the doc's sharpest distinction — gathering vs. collecting — is our whole problem stated cleanly:

modewhat happensresult
Collectingparts accumulated, meaning imposed from outsidea catalog; silos
Gatheringthings brought into relation and shelteredliving connections; meaning emerges from the relations

We've been collecting — cards, organs, silos, built and stacked. We never gathered them; we never ran the wires. Chorus dis-integrated is Chorus un-gathered. The one thing we failed to do to ourselves is the literal name of the product.

5 · What's actually left

The first draft of this section said "Connect. One verb." — and Silas was right to flag it on read: that is the grand-framing costume Jeff warns about, an essay standing in for a fix. So, decomposed into the three concrete wires it actually is:

#wireowner
aThe inject fires on peer tool-turn deltas, not on the role's own next prompt — the partner's motion reaches idle context (the observations.jsonl piece). This is what makes the rope taut.Wren / Kade
bRetire the /tmp-snapshot + cron-tick scaffolding under gemba/pair; read the live pulse.* slice directly.Wren (loom) — #3205
cinferred_stale computes actually-behind (data delta vs. the live object), not last-fidget. Kills the months of false "idle."Silas (index-freshness/observe) — carding it

Three wires, not one abstract verb. None of them is new construction — each connects parts that already work.

small   The concrete next step, named tonight as pulse-inject — the short-term-memory twin of chorus-inject:

Route the relevant peer's tool-turn stream through the per-prompt inject that is already running. In pair, the driver's turns land in the navigator as they happen; in gemba, the watched role's turns; for general awareness, the WIP/state deltas. New state arrives in the session as it occurs — push, not poll — and the cost is bounded by sending deltas, not full dumps (a full re-dump every turn is the torrent, and the failure). It rides the hook whose cadence matches the memory's volatility: SessionStart for the slow stuff, UserPromptSubmit for the working buffer, PreToolUse for discipline at the moment of action, PreCompact to re-anchor.

This is Wren's lane (memory, loom, the context/inject service) with Silas's attention model underneath it. It's filed as #3205 — not as a build card, since the parts exist, but as the wiring: collapse gemba + pair + mutual awareness onto one poll-pulse.* loom verb, retire the start/tick/cron/snapshot scaffolding.

A closing note, in plain terms, because the discussion earned it: none of this is a deficit story. The hard, expensive part — building the senses, the spine, the memory, the awareness layer — is done, and it's been done since spring. The vision was clear in mid-March and it has held. What remains is the unglamorous middle that rewards no one and therefore got skipped: the gathering. The integration. The wiring between organs that already work. That's why it's possible — you don't have to invent it into being. You have to connect it.
