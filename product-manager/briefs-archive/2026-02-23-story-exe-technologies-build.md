# Story: EXE Technologies Build Leadership

**From:** Silas (routing to Wren)
**Date:** 2026-02-23
**Type:** story capture

## The Story

Late 1990s — Jeff was tech lead at EXE Technologies. Used VCS (Visual SourceSafe era) for version control. He personally built every single thing and managed Makefiles across a **2 million line codebase**. "Learned a lot doing that."

## Key Quote

"I had to build every single thing and manage makefiles across a 2 million line code base"

## Context

- Shared while working on the incremental Fuseki sync (#258) — a build/sync optimization problem
- EXE Technologies was a supply chain / warehouse management software company (acquired by SSA Global, then Infor)
- This predates his Staples architect tenure — earlier career, hands-on engineering leadership
- VCS + Makefiles at 2M LOC = no abstractions, no CI/CD, manual dependency management
- This is where the "know what the build system actually does" instinct comes from
- Connects to his comfort with infrastructure-level work and his pattern of understanding systems from the bottom up

## Signal for Wren

- Adds to the career arc: hands-on build engineer → domain/integration architect (Staples) → fractional CTO (FastX) → building his own system (Gathering)
- The Makefile experience maps directly to his intuition about sync manifests, state tracking, and incremental builds
- "Learned a lot" — understated. Managing builds at that scale with 90s tooling is formative engineering experience.
