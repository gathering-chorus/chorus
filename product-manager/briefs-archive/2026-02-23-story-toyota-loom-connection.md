## The Story

Jeff pointed out that Toyota started as a loom manufacturer — Toyoda Automatic Loom Works. Much of lean manufacturing came from that lineage. He connected this to The Loom as a team name candidate.

## Key Quote

"You know that Toyota started as a loom manufacturer - much of lean came from that company"

## Context

- Shared during team name discussion — Silas had proposed The Forge and The Loom
- The original jidoka concept (autonomation — machines that stop themselves on error) was invented for looms: Sakichi Toyoda's automatic loom stopped when a thread broke
- Jeff's deploy freeze, health gates, quality gates, pre-commit hooks — all jidoka. The system stops itself when something's wrong.
- This reframes The Loom: it's not just "three threads, one weaver" — it carries the entire lean/Toyota lineage that Jeff already operates from (failure demand, jidoka, gemba walks, single piece flow)
- The Loom → Toyota → lean → jidoka → deploy freeze. The metaphor is load-bearing.
